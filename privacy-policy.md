# SpoilerShield Privacy Policy

_Last updated: May 2026_

SpoilerShield ("the extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data the extension accesses and how it is used.

## Data We Collect

**We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.**

SpoilerShield does not collect:
- Personal information (name, email, etc.)
- Financial or payment information
- Authentication credentials
- Personal communications
- Location data
- Web browsing history
- User activity (clicks, keystrokes, etc.)

## Data Stored Locally

The extension stores the following data **locally on your device only**, using Chrome's local storage:

- **Your selected NBA teams** — the teams you choose to block spoilers for.
- **Cached NBA team data** — rosters, schedules, team names, and logos fetched from the public ESPN API, used to detect spoiler content.
- **Extension settings** — such as whether the extension is enabled.

This data never leaves your device and is never sent to us or any third party.

## Third-Party Requests

The extension fetches publicly available NBA data (team rosters, schedules, logos) from ESPN's public API (`site.api.espn.com` and `a.espncdn.com`). These are read-only requests for sports data. No personal or user-identifying information is included in these requests.

## YouTube Content

The extension reads video titles, descriptions, and metadata on YouTube pages you visit in order to detect and blur potential spoilers. This processing happens entirely in your browser. None of this content is collected, stored, or transmitted off your device.

## Data Sharing

We do not sell, share, or transmit any user data to any third party, because we do not collect any.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page.

## Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please contact us through the Chrome Web Store listing.
